9.1 Inner Products

What makes standard basis easy to use?

  1. All the vectors in the basis are orthogonal
  2. All vectors in the basis have unit length

We have only defined the concept of orthogonality and length in Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn, our goal now is to generalize this to general vector spaces

Definition - Inner Product: Let V\mathbb{V}V be a vector space. An inner product on V\mathbb{V}V is a function ⟨,⟩:V×V→R\langle , \rangle : \mathbb{V} \times \mathbb{V} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}⟨,⟩:V×V→R such that for all v⃗,w⃗,z⃗∈V\vec{v}, \vec{w}, \vec{z} \in \mathbb{V}v,w,z∈V and a,b∈Ra, b \in \mathbb{R}a,b∈R we have

  1. ⟨v⃗,v⃗⟩≥0\langle \vec{v}, \vec{v} \rangle \geq 0⟨v,v⟩≥0. Also ⟨v⃗,v⃗⟩=0\lang \vec{v}, \vec{v} \rangle = 0⟨v,v⟩=0 if and only if v⃗=0⃗\vec{v} = \vec{0}v=0
  2. ⟨v⃗,w⃗⟩=⟨w⃗,v⃗⟩\langle \vec{v}, \vec{w} \rangle = \langle \vec{w}, \vec{v} \rangle⟨v,w⟩=⟨w,v⟩
  3. ⟨av⃗+bw⃗,z⃗⟩=a⟨v⃗,z⃗⟩+b⟨w⃗,z⃗⟩\langle a \vec{v} + b \vec{w}, \vec{z} \rangle = a \langle \vec{v}, \vec{z} \rangle + b \langle \vec{w}, \vec{z} \rangle⟨av+bw,z⟩=a⟨v,z⟩+b⟨w,z⟩

a function with property 1 is said to be positive definite
a function with property 2 is said to be symmetric
a function with property 3 is said to be left linear

if it is has both property 1 and 2 then it is also right linear and is said to be bilinear
